WCAG-focused examples

Accessible media patterns for mave-player

A focused set of examples for audio track selection, captions, and transcripts. These patterns map to common WCAG checkpoints and are ready to reuse in demos or docs.

Theme

Quick checks

  • Tab through player controls and confirm visible focus.
  • Toggle captions and verify legibility against the video.
  • Switch audio tracks and confirm labels are meaningful.
Tip: Use Tab, Space, and Enter to test controls.
WCAG 1.2.1 and 1.2.3

Audio track selection

When multiple tracks exist, the audio track menu stays visible and lets users switch between language or description tracks.

What to verify

  • Audio track menu is reachable by keyboard.
  • Track names are descriptive (language or role).
  • Switching tracks does not reset playback.

Attributes in use

audiotracks="on" controls="... audiotracks ..." color="#f6ead9"

WCAG 1.2.2

Captions enabled on load

Use subtitles and active-subtitle to display captions immediately, and keep the captions control visible for users who want to switch or disable them.

WCAG 1.4.3

High contrast (emulated)

This example forces the same tokens we use when prefers-contrast: more is enabled, without requiring an OS setting change.

What to verify

  • Icons and labels remain readable over bright frames.
  • Big play button stays legible on pause.
  • Menus retain contrast in hover and focus states.

Attributes in use

class="contrast-sim" subtitles="en"

WCAG 1.4.10

Compact player (~300px)

Players embedded in tight layouts should keep a usable control bar without clipping or overlapping controls.

What to verify

  • Controls wrap cleanly without overlapping.
  • Seek bar stays full width and usable.
  • Primary actions remain reachable by keyboard.

Attributes in use

width: 300px controls=\"...\"
